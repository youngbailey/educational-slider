Silent Film

Spanning from the 1890s to the 1920s, silent film marked the beginning of cinema as we know it. Contradictory to the name, most films of this time were accompanied by a score of either recorded or live music. Tom Gunning refers to this era as the "Cinema of Attractions," in which film was more of a spectacle rather having narratives. The film above is "Workers Leaving the Lumiere Factory" and is often considered the first real motion picture.

Sound Film

While film was always accompanied by sound, it wasn't until the 1920's that "talkies" emerged. Sound with dialogue was difficult to achieve due to the syncronization of sound and film. In 1819, sound-on-film was developed, in which the film strips photographically recorded the optical sound on the film itself. Additionally in this era, films were recognized by their production companies, not actors. The film above "Casablanca," was created by Warner Bros to influence the United States to disregard their neutral stance in World War II.

Coloring

Film colorization was mainly achieved in two ways: tinting and toning. This process was used since the silent era, but quickly lost popularity in the 1920s due to the fragility of film. In the 1950's, MGM created a new toning process that utilized special film cameras and an intereference-free process. The most well known example of this is technicolor which was used for films like the Wizard of Oz. The film above, "The Umbrellas of Cherbourg" is well known for its colorization which utilizes technicolor.

Modern Film

One major aspect of modern film today is digital cameras, but the industry didn't fully move away from film cameras until 2013. The 2010's presented a new era of technology and application to cinema, most notably including 3D movies. Additionally, the introduction of new streaming technologies creates the question of the proper way to experience film. This modern era also displayed the rise of the superhero genre when "Avengers: Endgame" became the highest grossing film of all time. Globalization has also created a rise in popularity of international films. In 2019, "Parasite" became the first non-English film to win Best Picture at the Academy Awards.
